# Responsible AI Principles Map

| Principle | What it means in practice | Related governance artifact |
|---|---|---|
| Accountability | A named owner is responsible for the AI use case, approval conditions, and ongoing review | AI use-case register; AI system inventory |
| Transparency | Users understand the purpose, limits, data sources, and review requirements of the AI system | AI system card; model card |
| Fairness | The AI system is reviewed for bias, disparate impact, and inappropriate variation across groups | AI risk assessment; bias review fields |
| Reliability | Outputs are tested, monitored, and reviewed against source data or expected business rules | Monitoring controls; data readiness checklist |
| Privacy | Personal and sensitive data are classified, minimized, and protected | Privacy/security checklist; access controls |
| Security | AI tools and vendors are reviewed for cyber, access, and misuse risks | Vendor review checklist; prompt controls |
| Human oversight | People remain accountable for decisions and review outputs before action | Human oversight SOP |
| Traceability | Inputs, outputs, decisions, and sources can be reviewed and audited | System inventory; model card; RAG checklist |
