# Responsible AI and GenAI Governance Module

This module extends the data governance control tower with a practical responsible AI and generative AI governance layer.

It demonstrates how an organization can register AI use cases, assess risk, evaluate data readiness, document model/system controls, review vendor and privacy/security risks, and monitor AI-related issues before AI systems are approved or scaled.

## What this module covers

- AI use-case intake and approval workflow
- AI system inventory
- NIST AI RMF mapping
- NIST Generative AI Profile mapping
- Generative AI risk register
- Data readiness for AI checklist
- AI risk assessment
- Privacy and security review checklist
- Vendor review checklist
- Model card and AI system card templates
- Human oversight and escalation procedure
- AI governance control library
- RAG governance checklist
- AI issue and incident log

## Governance purpose

The goal is to show how responsible AI governance can be operationalized through concrete artifacts rather than remaining only at the policy level.

This module connects data governance foundations such as ownership, metadata, lineage, quality, classification, access control, issue management, and stewardship to AI-specific risks such as hallucination, bias, privacy exposure, lack of traceability, overreliance, model drift, and vendor risk.
